Prepping the Plants

PART TWO


Next, we’re going to prepare the flower before it is pressed. If you can’t press the flower right away, put it in a ziplock bag and store it in a refrigerator. To maintain the color and freshness of the flower, we are going to:

  1. Cut stems at a 45˚ angle, and place immediately into water.
  2. Remove leaves that are lower than the waterline, as they will collect unwanted bacteria.
  3. Add into vase with water.
  4. Add flower food or a teaspoon of sugar.
  5. Keep in a cool ventilated place for a few hours.
  6. If using thicker flowers (such as a rose),
    cut in half, so it will fold flatly.
