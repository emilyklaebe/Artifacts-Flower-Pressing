Ways to Press

PART THREE


Finally, we can use two different ways to press botanicals: the wooden press or books. Both have their advantages, and can make beautiful pressed plants.

WOODEN FLOWER PRESS:
  1. Cut two pieces of plywood; drill holes in each board.
  2. Layer flowers between two pieces of paper and wood.
  3. Use wingnuts and bolts to tighten everything together.
  4. Blotter papers will need to be changed every four days or so; press plants for 3-4 weeks.
BOOK PRESS:
  1. Use heavy books, like dictionary or phone books. Use a book you don’t mind wrinkling, the moisture being absorbed has that affect.
  2. Layer the flowers between paper.
  3. You may be able to press multiple flowers in one book. Space flowers out, so the moisture doesn’t transfer between flowers.
  4. Weigh down book with weights, other books or even a brick.
  5. Change blotter papers every few days. Flowers will be dry after 2-3 weeks. Remove flowers with tweezers.
