Choosing the Right Botanical

PART ONE


With this stage, we are looking for plants that are sturdy and either just bloomed or ending their time. Freshness is the key to ensuring a long-lasting final result. If possible, make sure to find botanicals that are freshly fallen, or directly cut from a garden. When picking from a garden, the best time to cut flowers or plants is the morning after dew has evaporated. Flowers will naturally flat faces are easiest to press, due to their natural fold.
